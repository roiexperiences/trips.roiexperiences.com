Nestled right outside the Grand Teton National Park and less than 55 miles from Yellowstone National Park in the northwest corner of Wyoming lies Jackson Hole. The Jackson Hole area stretches 55 miles in a low-lying valley surrounded by mountains resulting in the perfect habitat for wildlife and stunning views. The Jackson Hole airport is conveniently located within the Grand Teton National Park making your journey to Jackson Hole, Wyoming seamless. Jackson Hole offers a plethora of activities for all outdoor enthusiasts, including breathtaking scenic tours, world class dining, amazing skiing and snowboarding opportunities, unique art, and unmatched shopping. The summer season in Jackson Hole offers hiking, backpacking, camping, whitewater rafting, fishing, horseback riding, and enjoying the hot springs, in addition to incredible wildlife tours. In the winter, you can enjoy skiing, snowshoeing, dog sledding, snowmobiling, and skating, along with other unique opportunities. Located in the center of town is the Jackson Hole Town Square which features a park surrounded by shops, restaurants, retailers, and the frequently photographed iconic antler arches.



YELLOWSTONE

On March 1, 1872, Yellowstone became the first national park. Yellowstone National Park spans an area of 3,468.4 sq mi (8,983 km2), comprising lakes, canyons, rivers, and mountain ranges. Yellowstone lake is one of the largest high-elevation lakes in North America and is centered over the Yellowstone Caldera, the largest supervolcano on the continent. The caldera is considered a dormant volcano. It has erupted with tremendous force several times in the last two million years. Well over half of the world's geysers and hydrothermal features are in Yellowstone, fueled by this ongoing volcanism.
